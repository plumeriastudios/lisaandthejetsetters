About the Dominican Republic

The Dominican Republic offers a wide range of experiences - from all-inclusive beaches to historic Santo Domingo and inland adventures - making it a flexible choice for new travelers.

Local tip: Bring some small cash in Dominican pesos for local markets and tipping. Many tourist areas accept USD but locals often expect pesos for small purchases.

Important forms & resources

  • Dominican Republic e‑Ticket (official entry form) — complete as required before travel.
  • Go Dominican Republic — official tourism site for attractions and local operators.
  • Viator — recommended for booking tours (check operator reviews).

Top things to do

  • Relax at beaches in Punta Cana or Samaná.
  • Explore Zona Colonial (Santo Domingo) for history and restaurants.
  • Take day trips to El Limón waterfall or Los Haitises National Park.
  • I did the Monkey Land excursion which started with ziplines and then some off-roading to a beautiful waterfall - an incredible adventure you don't want to miss!
  • Enjoy local music, dance, and coffee culture.

Places to eat

  • La Casita de Don Alfredo (Santo Domingo) - Traditional Dominican cuisine with mangu and sancocho.
  • Adrian Tropical (Santo Domingo, Malecon) - Beachfront restaurant known for fresh seafood and mofongo.
  • Jellyfish Beach Restaurant (Punta Cana) - Caribbean cuisine with coconut shrimp and ceviche.
  • El Conuco (Santo Domingo) - Cultural experience with live music, chicharron, and tostones.
  • Street food stands - Try local chimichangas (Dominican burgers), yaroa, and fresh tropical juices.
  • Passion by Martin Berasategui (Punta Cana) - Fine dining for special occasions.

Pro tip: Most restaurants accept USD, but having Dominican pesos for smaller local spots is helpful. Tipping 10-15% is customary for good service.
